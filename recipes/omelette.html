Omelette

Follow this recipe and you will be able to make a delisious omellete as the one pictured above.

This recipe is one of the simplest, and most delicious breakfast/brunch meal I know. There are a few alternative adition you can make but the basis stay pretty much the same. The key to making the propper french omelette is to no over cook it. There is this golden middle ground here. Not too hard and not too soft. it is important to take it slow and stay atentive. When browning starts to form on the outside of the omellete it changes the flavour and ceases to be a french omelette. Some twists you could add to the omelette is, for example, blue cheese and caramalized onions.

Ingredients

  • 6 egs
  • 25gr unsalted butter
  • chives
  • Two pinches of grated parmesan cheese

Discription

  1. Heat the pan on low heat
  2. beat the eggs for aproximatly 3 min with a fork or 1 min to with a wisk
  3. Add the butter to the pan. No need to wait on browning cause a butter taste is what you want with your egg
  4. add the eggs and stur slowly. Think of sturrinf as if youd make a mixed egg but slower. The idea is to have the egg cook evenly and stay reasonably unbrowned at the bottem.
